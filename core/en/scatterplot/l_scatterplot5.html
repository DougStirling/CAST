World population

A time series plot can be thought of as a scatterplot of the variable against time.

Click on crosses to illustrate how the positions on the two axes correspond to the year and population increase.

The data set shows the percentage increase in the world population between each year and the previous year between 1950 and 2001.

  • There were considerable fluctuations between 1950 and 1960.
  • The percentage increase dropped fairly steadily after 1960.
  • Of course, the percentage increase was still positive in 2001 so the world population was still increasing!
