Properties of 95% CI

As with earlier confidence intervals, the 95% CI for σ is random and varies from sample to sample.

  • If we had the luxury of being able to obtain repeated random samples, 95% of them would result in CIs that include σ. (In practice however, we only have a single sample.)
  • We may be unlucky and the confidence interval that we obtain from a single sample data set may not include σ.
  • We are 95% confident that our CI will include σ.
