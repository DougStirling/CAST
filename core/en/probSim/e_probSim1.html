When there are a finite number of possible outcomes and all are equally likely, it is relatively easy to find the probability of any event — the probability is simply the proportion of possible outcomes for which the event occurs.

Random outcomes that are equally likely are a critical component of many games of chance.

There are two types of question that may be asked in the exercise below.

  • In one type of question, two dice are rolled and a table shows the 36 possible combinations of values.
  • In the other type of question, one card is picked from a standard pack of playing cards and the table shows all 52 combinations of suit and card value.

In both variations, read the question carefully to determine which of the equally likely outcomes are being referred to, and type their probability in the answer box.

Repeat with a few different questions.
