Evaluation of 95% CIs

In practice, a computer is often used to evaluate 95% confidence intervals. However the steps that are followed to evaluate the intervals are also important in their own right, so it is useful to get some practice at evaluating confidence intervals by hand.

The exercises below give practice with the calculations for a 95% confidence interval for the difference between two group means.

For the data set below, evaluate a 95% confidence interval for the difference between the population means. Use the green templates on the right of the diagram to help with your calculations.

  • For each group separately, estimate the standard deviation of the group mean. (Type the group standard deviation and sample size in the boxes and hit the Return key.)
  • Combine the two standard deviations to find the standard deviation of the difference between the means.
  • Type the degrees of freedom and hit Return to obtain the appropriate t-value.
  • Use these values to complete the confidence interval, then click Check.

Repeat with different data sets until you feel confident about the method.
