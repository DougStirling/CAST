Bar charts for samples from a categorical population

Use the diagram to show that a sample bar chart becomes less variable as the sample size increases and that the bar heights tend towards the probabilities of the categories. (This is a consequence of the law of large numbers.)

  • Take a few samples to observe variability.
  • Increase the sample size and repeat.
  • The 'infinite sample' barchart gives the population proportions (probabilities).

Mention that the (limiting) bar chart of probabilities is the most common way to display an infinite categorical or discrete population.
