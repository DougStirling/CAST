CI for µ when σ is known: simulation from normal (µ = 12, σ = 2)

This diagram shows that changing the constant k from 1.96 changes the proportion of CIs that include µ.

Click Accumulate and take 100 or more samples. Now adjust k.

  • Reducing k decreases the width of the confidence intervals, so fewer include µ = 12.
  • When k = 1, about 2/3 of the intervals include µ = 12. The theoretical confidence level is 0.683.
  • When k = 3, all (or almost all) of the intervals include µ = 12. The theoretical confidence level is 0.997.
