Uncertainty

There are many situations in which we do not know precisely what will happen.

Examples

  • Will it be raining at 12 noon tomorrow?
  • If I pick a card from a shuffled deck, will it be a spade?
  • How long will it take me to drive to work?
  • Who will win the men's and women's tennis finals at Wimbledon next year?
  • Will I get a serious disease in the next ten years?
  • Will my baggage go missing on my next flight?

However with current knowledge, there is no choice but to treat the outcome as being random, with various possible outcomes.
