P-value

The p-value is the probability of such an 'extreme' value of the test statistic when H0 is true. This is the upper tail area of the F distribution,

This p-value is interpreted in exactly the same way as other p-values:

  • The smaller the p-value, the stronger the evidence that the null hypthesis does not hold — i.e. that µ is not equal to k.
  • A large p-value (say 0.1 or higher) means that the data are consistent with µ being equal to k.
