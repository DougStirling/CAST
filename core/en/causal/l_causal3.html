In a sample of farms, rainfall (including irrigation from sprinklers) and crop yield are measured.

Irrigation and wheat growth: an experiment

Moisture is controlled using supplementary irrigation. Three fields in each of 8 farms are used with moisture topped up to 2, 2.5 and 3 mm per day.

Use the pop-up menu to show the temperature in each field. In the observational study,

  • Wetter farms are also colder, countering any benefits from higher rainfall.
  • Yield and rainfall are positively related within each temperature group.

In the experiment,

  • Each irrigation level is used in the same 8 farms so it is not related to temperature.
  • Yield and moisture have the same relationship overall and within each temperature group.

In the observational study, rain gauges are installed in a sample of farms. Rainfall (which includes irrigation from sprinklers) and crop yield are subsequently measured.
