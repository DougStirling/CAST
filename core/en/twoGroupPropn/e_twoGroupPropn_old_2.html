This page provides some exercises that give practice with evaluating and interpreting p-values. As in the previous exercises about evaluating confidence intervals, we strongly recommend using statistical software to perform hypothesis tests in practice. However there are important general principles that are reinforced by performing a few exercises by hand.

Exercises

In the exercises below, you will perform a mixture of one-tailed and two-tailed hypothesis tests.

  • Use the green templates on the right to separately estimate the standard deviations of the proportions of 'successes' in both groups.
  • Combine these standard deviations to estimate the standard deviation of their difference.
  • Standardise the difference between the sample proportions to find the z-value for the test.
  • Drag over the standard normal curve to read off the lower tail area corresponding to z.
  • Use the tail area to evaluate the appropriate p-value, then select your conclusion.
  • Click the button Check.

Repeat with different questions until you feel confident with the method.
