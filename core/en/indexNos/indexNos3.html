Several items

An aggregate price index combines the prices of several related items into a single index number. The group of items is sometimes referred to as a market basket or basket for short.

There are many examples of aggregate indices â€“ the NZX50 index aggregates the prices of the top 50 companies (as measured by market capitalisation) listed on the New Zealand Stock Exchange, the quarterly Consumers Price Index (or CPI) aggregates the prices of a range of food and related household shopping items and is commonly used as a measure for price inflation in the New Zealand economy.
