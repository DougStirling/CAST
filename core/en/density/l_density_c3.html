Lengths of telephone calls

Use the Wider and Narrower buttons to show that:

  • When class width is less than 1.0, the histogram starts to look jagged
  • When class width is greater than about 2.0, the histogram becomes blocky.

Any class width between 1.0 and 2.0 would be acceptable.

Use the Left and Right buttons to show that the starting-point for the first class also affects histogram shape but is less important than the class width.

Mention that if your conclusion from a histogram depends on the choice of classes, you are over-interpreting it.

The data set shows the lengths of 200 long-distance telephone calls (mins) from a company's head office.
