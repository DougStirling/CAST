Blocks

In many experiments, there is structure to the experimental units that is known before the experiment is started. This is often modelled by defining blocks of similar units. The blocks may be based on measured characteristics of the units or may describe a more fundamental structure of the units.

  • An agricultural experiment may use several fields on the same or different farms. Each field is split into several smaller plots from which crop yield is measured. The plots are the experimental units and the fields are blocks.
  • An animal experiment may involve several herds of cattle (blocks) but measurements are made on individual cows.
  • In an experiment about preserving fish meat from a fish farm, a few samples may be obtained from each fish. The meat samples are the experimental units and the fish are the blocks.

Factors varied at unit level

In earlier chapters, it has been possible to apply (and vary) treatments at the level of the experimental units:

  • Different crops can be planted in different plots within a field.
  • Different worming treatments can be applied to different animals.
  • Different preservatives can be applied to different fish-meat samples.

Factors varied at block level

In other types of experiment, it may not be possible to vary a factor within each block.

  • Irrigation can only be applied to whole fields and cannot be varied between plots within a field.
  • Rotation of cattle between paddocks can only be varied at herd level — all cows in a herd must get the same rotation regime.
  • The way that the fish are captured and killed can be varied at fish level — all meat samples from the same fish have the same capture and killing method.

Analysis of variance

In a conventional analysis of variance table, the effect of the blocks is removed first and the further reduction to the residual sum of squares when the factor is added to the model (the sum of squares explained by the factor after the blocks) is used to test the effect of the factor.

If the factor is varied at block level, this results in an analysis of variance table with zero degrees of freedom for the factor, so the effect of the factor cannot be tested.
