Australia Post claims to deliver 96% of letters on time but only 52 out of 59 letters arrived on time in a study.

In this simulation, the null hypothesis is that the 96% of letters arrive on time:

  • P(on time) = 0.96

Click Accumulate and simulate attempts to deliver 59 letters between 100 and 200 times. (Hold down the Simulate button.)

Getting only 52 letters delivered on time would be unlikely if Australia Post's claim was correct so there is strong evidence that fewer than 96% are delivered on time.

Emphasise the general concepts:

Null hypothesis, H0
Each of the letters has P(on time) = 0.96
Alternative hypothesis, HA
P(on time) < 0.96
Test statistic
Number delivered on time
P-value
Probability of getting such a low test statistic if H0 is true
Conclusion
A small p-value gives evidence that H0 is not true.
