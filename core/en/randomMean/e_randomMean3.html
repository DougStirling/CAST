This exercise expects you to identify how the distribution of a sample mean, sample sum or a single sampled value differs from the population distribution in terms of centre, spread and skewness.

In this exercise, the population has a skew distribution. Use the pop-up menus to specify the distribution of the sample statistic specified in the question.

Repeat with a few different questions until you are confident that you know how the distribution of sample means and sums differ in shape from the population distribution.
