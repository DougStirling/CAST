Zinc and colds

An experiment was conducted on volunteers who developed a cold within the previous 24 hours. They were randomised to take either zinc acetate lozenges or placebo lozenges (identical but with no active ingredient). The duration of cold symptoms for the subjects who received zinc had mean 4.5 days, and standard deviation1.6 days. The control group's duration had mean 8.1 days, and standard deviation 1.8 days.

The simulation uses these values as population parameters.
