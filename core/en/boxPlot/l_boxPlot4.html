Outlier or skew distribution?


Definition of 'outliers'

Use the slider on the top diagram to explain that a basic box plot cannot distinguish between an outlier and a skew distribution. It cannot show whether the maximum (or minimum) is:

  • a correct values that is simply in the long tail of a skew distribution, or
  • a value that is unlike any other in the data set.

The second diagram explains a common definition of an 'outlier' that is displayed as a separate cross — more than 1.5IQR outside the quartiles. Drag the highest cross to turn it into an outlier and show how:

  • it becomes displayed as a separate cross, and
  • the 'whisker' of the box plot only stretches to the next biggest value within the 1.5IQR limits.

All crosses can be dragged in the diagram so you could also illustrate the fact that 2 or more crosses can be separately displayed as crosses with this rule.

Mention that this is a crude definition of an 'outlier' and it must not be immediately concluded that there is anything wrong with such a value.
