Earlier chapters examined the distribution of values in single variables and the relationship between pairs of variables. However many data sets contain three or more measurements from each individual.

Furthermore these measurements may be numerical or categorical. We introduce the ideas of modelling these more realistic, though also more complex situations.

We also introduce the logistic model where the variable of interest is binomial and we wish to model its variability in terms of other measurements, just as we have done earlier for normally distributed data.
