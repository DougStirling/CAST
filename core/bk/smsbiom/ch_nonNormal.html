In this Chapter, like Topic 8 in e-sms, we first review why the normal distribution is so important in statistics. Then we look particularly at data that come from asking yes/no or true/false questions. These are often called binomial data. They are a special case of categorical data, e.g. data on a five-point scale (very bad, to very good), where there are just 2 categories.

Chapter 6 in this CAST book has shown how categorical data can be summarised and we we move here to statistical inference where, as in Topic/Chapters 3 and 4, we assume the data are a sample from some population of interest.

Modelling in this way allows us to better assess features in the data. In particular, we can assess the precision of summary statistics, such as a sample proportion.
