When only a single measurement is made from each individual (or unit) in a group, we cannot associate variation in that measurement with other characteristics of the individuals — all variation is unexplained.

When two or more measurements are made from each individual, we may be be able to associate variation in one measurement with changes in the other measurements; this can explain some of the variation. Examples are ...

  • Annual salary and years of experience of accountants in a large accounting company
  • Floor area and sale price of houses in a town
  • Sales of cameras and advertising expenditure by a chain of photographic shops in each of 20 weeks

Our aim with such data is to find information about the relationship between the variables. We use this example of a simple (regression) model also to show how the modelling process makes use of the ideas of estimation and testing that were discussed in the two previous Chapters/Topics.
