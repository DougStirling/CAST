Data may have a rich structure, but we first concentrate on the simplest data structure, a single numerical measurement from each 'individual' in a group.

The exercises in this chapter will help you to learn about:

  • Graphical displays — stem and leaf plots, dot plots, box plots and histograms
  • Numerical summaries of centre and spread — means, medians and standard deviations
  • Proportions and percentiles
  • Linear and nonlinear transformations
