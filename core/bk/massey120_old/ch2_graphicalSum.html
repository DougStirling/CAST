Data may have a rich structure, but we first concentrate on the simplest data structure, a single measurement from each 'individual' in a group. We call data of this form a batch of values, or a univariate data set.

Examples of the types of 'individuals' from which we often record data are ...

  • People
  • Plants
  • Samples of output from a manufacturing process
  • Admissions of patients to a hospital

In this chapter, we describe some simple ways to display the variability in this kind of data set.
