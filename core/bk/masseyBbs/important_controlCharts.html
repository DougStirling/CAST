What you need to know

You will not be examined about everything in this chapter. Some of the material has been included to explain why the chapter's methods are used, in the hope that it will help you to understand these methods better. What you need to learn for the exam is more limited.

We now describe what we expect you to be able to do in the exam after studying the control charts chapter.

A.   Total quality management

1.   Plan-Do-Check-Act cycle
  • Name the cycle.
  • Explain the four parts to the cycle and give an example.
2.   Brainstorming
Explain what brainstorming is intended for, and how it operates.
3.   Cause-and-effect diagrams
Explain how cause-and-effect diagrams are structured.

B.   Control charts

Control charts track the preformance of processes over time.

1.   Types of variation
  • Explain the difference between common cause and special cause variation.
  • Explain when a process is in control and out of control.
  • Explain what training data is for.
2.   Control chart limits
Calculate the upper and lower control limits for:
  • Sample means
  • Sample ranges
  • Sample proportions
3.   Assess whether a process is in control
For all three types of control chart,
  • Draw the control limits on the control chart.
  • Identify whether values are outside the control limits.
  • Identify whether there are runs of 6 increasing or decreasing.
  • Identify whether 9 successive points are on one side of the centre.
  • Describe what these 'triggers' indicate about the process.
