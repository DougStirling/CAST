95% confidence interval for a proportion

An approximate 95% confidence interval for a population proportion (probability) has the form

p   ±   1.96

The constant 1.96 is used because the sample proportion, p, is approximately normally distributed and any normal distribution has 95% probability of being within 1.96 standard deviations of its mean.

Different confidence levels

For a higher confidence level, the confidence interval must be widened. A narrower confidence interval similarly results in a lower confidence level.

We therefore now consider interval estimates of the form

p   ±   k

where k can take values other than 1.96. The relationship between k and the confidence level is determined by the standard normal distribution,

The table below gives some examples of k and the resulting confidence level.

k Confidence level
1 0.683
2 0.954
3 0.997
1.645 0.90
1.960 0.95
2.576 0.99

Although 95% confidence intervals are most commonly reported, sometimes k is chosen to give a 90% or 99% confidence interval.
