Categorical populations

For categorical populations, the unknowns are the population probabilities for the different categories. We focus on one category that is of particular interest ('success').

The null and alternative hypotheses are specified in terms of the probability of success, π. If the alternative hypothesis includes both high and low values of π, the test is two-tailed, such as

H0:   π = 0.5
HA:   π ≠ 0.5

If the alternative hypothesis only allows low (or high) values of π, the test is one-tailed

H0:   π = 0.96
HA:   π < 0.96
