Normal approximation to the error distribution

If the sample size, n, is large enough, the distribution of p is approximately normal (by the Central Limit Theorem) so

error  =  p − π  ~  normal (0, )

Closeness of the normal approximation

The diagram below shows the exact distribution for the errors (based on the binomial distribution) when sampling with probability π of success (red). The above normal approximation is shown in grey.

Use the sliders to verify that

  • The normal approximation improves as n increases, whatever the value of π.
  • The normal approximation is best when π is close to 0.5.

The normal approximation to the error distribution is therefore reasonable provided the sample size is reasonably large and π is not close to 0 or 1. (We will give better guidelines later.)
