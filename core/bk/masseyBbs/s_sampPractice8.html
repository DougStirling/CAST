Stratified, cluster and two-stage sampling schemes have various advantages and disadvantages, compared to a sample random sample that records information from the same number of individuals.

Stratified sampling
A stratified random sample provides more accurate estimates of population characteristics than a simple random sample of the same size from the population. The advantage of stratified sampling is greatest when the strata differ in their mean value of the characteristic.
Cluster sampling
As mentioned earlier, it is usually much cheaper to obtain information from individuals in clusters than from the same number of individuals in a simple random sample. However the penalty is that the mean population characteristic is less accurately estimated.
Two-stage sampling
As with cluster sampling, two-stage sampling has lower cost than simple random sampling but usually estimates population characteristics less accurately.
