It is very difficult to get useful information from a list of the raw values in a numerical data set.

Graphical summaries of the data are particulary effective for highlighting the important information about a single numerical variable. Two particular aspects of its distribution of values — the centre and spread — are particularly important and numerical summaries of centre and spread are discussed.

For data sets containing two numerical variables, the relationship between the variables is also important. Graphical and numerical summaries of their relationship are described at the end of the chapter.
