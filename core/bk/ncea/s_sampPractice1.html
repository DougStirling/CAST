Although a simple random sample is conceptually the easiest sampling scheme, there are sometimes advantages to selecting a sample in different ways.

Separate samples from different groups

In a stratified random sample, the individuals in the population are split into different groups (called strata). A separate simple random sample is selected from each stratum.

If the strata differ from each other, a stratified random sample is more accurate than a simple random sample.

Cluster samples

It is often cheaper to sample groups of related individuals called clusters. For example, houses are often sampled with questions asked from all members of the selected households.

Although estimates will be less accurate than from a simple random sample of the same number of individuals, the reduced cost may allow more individuals to be sampled.

Systematic samples

A systematic sample is done when the individuals can be listed in order. For example, when sampling items for defects in the output of a production line, every 100th item may be inspected.

A systematic sample is easier to implement for the person collecting the data.
