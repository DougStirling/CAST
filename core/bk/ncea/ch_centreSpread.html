This chapter contains a single section that presents the mean and median of a numerical data set as 'typical' values. It also explains the importance of the variability of the values and describes simple measures of spread.

This material is mostly taught before Year 11 in New Zealand high schools so it should be mainly revision in Year 11. However some pages may provide extra information about specific methods since there is some variability in the coverage of topics in Years 9 and 10 between schools.

The material in this chapter can be used to support the teaching of statistical methods up to Year 10.

It can also be used for revision within an NCEA Level 1 programme.

The chapter is intended for self-study by students.

The material does not cover the complete curriculum. It has been provided as support material for parts of the curriculum.
