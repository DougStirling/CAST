This chapter explains some statistical concepts related to the NCEA level 3 standard "3.9: Investigate bivariate measurement data". It is usually taught at Year 13 in New Zealand high schools and is assessed at NCEA Level 3.

Earlier chapters of this e-book explained how to use and interpret scatterplots for bivariate data. This chapter extends this with a summary of the strength of the relationship between the variables and a method to predict the value of one variable from the other.

The chapter is intended for self-study by students.

Assumed background

This chapter assumes an understanding of bivariate data, scatterplots and relationships between variables. Two sections from earlier in this e-book can be used for revision of some of these ideas:

  • Scatterplots
  • Understanding relationships

Make sure that you fully understand these sections before starting this chapter.

The material does not cover the complete curriculum. It has been provided as support material for parts of the curriculum.
