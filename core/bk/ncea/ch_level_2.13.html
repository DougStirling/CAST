This chapter explains some statistical concepts related to the NCEA level 2 standard "2.13: Investigate a situation involving elements of chance using a simulation". It is usually taught in Year 12 in New Zealand high schools and is assessed at NCEA Level 2.

The chapter explains how probability models and similations can be used to gain insight into some random situations.

The chapter is intended for self-study by students.

Assumed background

The material in this section assumes a good understanding of probability, as described in the NCEA Level 1 section Probability. Make sure that you fully understand this material before reading further.

This chapter does not cover the complete curriculum. It has been provided as support material for parts of the curriculum.
