This chapter explains some statistical concepts related to the NCEA level 2 standard "2.9: Use statistical methods to make an inference".

Variation is a feature of most data sets. This variation is modelled in terms of random sampling from some underlying population. This chapter explains the consequences of this random variation and introduces the idea that a set of sample data can lead to estimation of population characteristics.

The chapter is intended for self-study by students.

Assumed background

The section Finite Populations at NCEA Level 1 of this e-book introduced the ideas of random sampling from finite populations. It should be revised before starting this section.

This chapter does not cover the complete curriculum. It has been provided as support material for parts of the curriculum.
