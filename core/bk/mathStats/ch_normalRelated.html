When data are recorded, we often assume that they arise as a random sample from a normal distribution. Three further standard distributions naturally arise to describe other variables that are defined in terms of normal random samples.

  • The variance of a normal random sample has a distribution that is proportional to a chi-squared distribution.
  • The difference between a normal sample's mean and the underlying distribution mean has a t distribution when divided by its standard error, provided this standard error is found using the sample variance.
  • The ratio of the variances from two normal samples has a distribution proportional to an F distribution.

These three distributions are of great practical importance since they underlie many commonly used confidence intervals and hypothesis tests. This chapter describes the distributions and their properties.
