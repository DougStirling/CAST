A box plot displays a five number summary of the distribution of values in a data set (median, quartiles, minimum and maximum) and usually gives a good overall impression of the distribution.

The mean and standard deviation summarise two important aspects of the distribution and are concise ways to compare different data sets.

Proportions and percentiles provide other numerical summaries that target meaningful aspects of a distribution. They can give more information about the tails of the distribution — for example, the occurrence of rare climatic conditions.
