The values in some data sets are recorded sequentially in time. Examples are

  • Average monthly house price in a city over a period of 5 years
  • Number of students enrolling at a university each year for the past 15 years
  • Strength of every 100th screwdriver manufactured during an 8-hour shift
  • Number of hospital admissions each day over a period of 5 months

In such time series data, there is often a time-related pattern to the variability. This may be either a trend towards higher or lower values over time or a pattern that repeats regularly. This aspect of overall variability may be explained in terms of the time-ordering of the data.

This chapter describes techniques to analyse patterns in time series and to use these patterns to forecast future values.
