This chapter describes the idea of using a random sample to obtain information about a population. Practical difficulties about conducting surveys are described.This material is usually taught in Year 12 in New Zealand high schools and is assessed at NCEA Level 2.

The chapter is intended for self-study by students.

Assumed background

This chapter only assumes an understanding of basic graphical and numerical summaries of numerical and categorical data that are usually taught at school before Year 11.

Make sure that you fully understand the pre-NCEA chapters of this e-book before starting work on the new material here.

The material does not cover the complete curriculum. It has been provided as support material for parts of the curriculum.
