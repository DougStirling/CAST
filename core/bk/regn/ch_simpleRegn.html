This chapter describes in detail how variation in a 'response' can be modelled in terms of a single explanatory variable. Inference about the model parameters gives an indication of the accuracy of the parameter estimates and predictions from the model. It also provides a test for whether the relationship is significant.

The simple linear regression model is not appropriate for all data sets.

  • There may be outliers which are harder to detect than in a univariate data set.
  • The relationship may be nonlinear.
  • One or more observations may have a disproportionate influence on the results.

The data should be carefully examined for these problems. Regression diagnostics are describes that help to detect them.
