Chapter 10   Coming Down from the Mountains

10.1   Some review exercises

10.1.1   Shape of a box plot

The exercises on this page involve matching the shape of box plots to the corresponding dot plots.

10.1.2   Information from distributions

In this exercise, you will interpret what distributions with symmetry, skewness, clusters or outliers tell you about the data.

10.1.3   Ideas of centre and spread

In this exercise, you should summarise the difference between two distributions in terms of their different centres and spreads.

10.1.4   Standard deviation from graph

The first exercise asks for a rough 'guess' at the standard deviation of data sets from a stacked dot plot. The second exercise is similar but displays the data as a histogram or box plot.

10.1.5   Factors affecting the standard deviation and standard error

This exercise asks 3 questions about the effect of sample size and spread of sample values on the sd and se, and about the interpretation of the se and sd.

10.1.6   Interpreting a 95% confidence interval

In this exercise, you must choose which of four statements correctly interprets the meaning of a 95% confidence interval.

10.1.7   Interpretation of p-values (harder)

This exercise is similar to the previous one but the wordings of the conclusions are harder.

10.2   A few more exercises!

10.2.1   Interpreting the slope and intercept

In this exercise, you are asked to select one of four statements that correctly describes the slope or intercept of a least squares line in the context of the data.

10.2.2   Standard error of slope

This exercise gives practice in evaluating a 95% confidence interval for a regression model's slope, based on its least squares estimate and standard error.

10.2.3   Regression problems

This exercise asks you to identify the difficulties with using the least squares line to predict Y at a given X from the data in a displayed scatterplot (an outlier, curvature, a high-leverage point or extrapolation).

10.2.4   Clusters and outliers (advanced)

The two exercises on this page ask the effect of combining different groups of values or adding an outlier on the mean and standard deviation.

10.2.5   Shape of distribution of mean and sum

This exercise examines understanding of the limiting normal distribution of the sample mean and sum as sample size increases, whatever the shape of the population distribution.

10.2.6   Distribution of mean and sum (cont)

In this exercise, pop-up menus are used to specify the centre, spread and skewness of the distribution of a sample mean, sample sum or a single value, given the population distribution.

10.2.7   Factors affecting width of confidence interval

This exercise asks how the sample size, confidence level and population probability affect the width of a CI for the probability.
