What affects the accuracy of a prediction?

Since the predicted response at X,

  =  b0 + b1 x

depends on the least squares estimates, b0 and b1, it also varies from sample to sample. The prediction has a normal distribution whose mean is

μy  =  β0  +  β1x

The standard deviation of the prediction describes its accuracy. It depends on:

Characteristics of the 'training' data
We saw earlier that the slope and intercept are estimated most accurately if the data have large n, small Ïƒ and a large spread of x-values.
The x-value at which a prediction is being made
Less obviously, the variability of the prediction also depends on the value of X at which the prediction is being made. The further X is from its mean in the training data, , the greater the variability in the prediction.

Predictions are least variable (most accurate) when predicting at an x-value near the mean of the 'training' data.
